Louisiana cosmetology license renewal happens every year, not every two years like most states, and it's paid online through PayStar, a third-party payment processor linked directly from the Louisiana State Board of Cosmetology's own website. There's no continuing education requirement attached, just the annual fee, which makes Louisiana's process shorter than most states' but easier to let slip past you precisely because it comes around every twelve months instead of every twenty-four.

The Louisiana State Board of Cosmetology, based in Baton Rouge, licenses and renews cosmetologists, estheticians, manicurists, and instructors, each on the same annual cycle but as separate license types with separate fees.

## How often do you renew a Louisiana cosmetology license?

Annually. That's the detail most people licensed in other states get wrong when they first deal with Louisiana, since the two-year cycle is the more common pattern nationally. Louisiana's board renews cosmetologist, esthetician, manicurist, and instructor licenses every year, which means a smaller renewal fee shows up on your calendar twice as often as it would somewhere on a standard biennial cycle.

## What does Louisiana cosmetology renewal cost?

The renewal fee for cosmetologists, estheticians, manicurists, and instructors is $35, effective August 1, 2025, according to a licensing fee tracker that monitors state cosmetology board fee schedules. Confirm the exact current figure on the board's own renewal form before paying, since fees change periodically and the board's own published number always overrides any third-party summary.

## How do you renew online through PayStar, step by step?

1. Go to the Louisiana State Board of Cosmetology's website and find the Payments section, which links directly to the board's PayStar payment page.
2. Have your license number ready. PayStar ties your payment to your specific license record, so an exact match matters.
3. Confirm you're within the renewal window. The board only opens online renewal 56 days before your license's expiration date, so if you try earlier, the system won't process the payment yet.
4. Enter your payment information. PayStar accepts Visa, MasterCard, American Express, and Discover only, no ACH or bank transfer option through the online portal.
5. Submit and keep your confirmation. The board's site doesn't instantly reflect payment status everywhere, so hold onto your payment confirmation until you can verify your license shows current.

If you'd rather not pay online, the board still accepts mailed renewals: download and print the license renewal form, include a money order, cashier's check, or personal check, and mail it to LSBC at 11622 Sunbelt Court, Baton Rouge, LA 70809. Mail renewal takes longer to process and doesn't give you the same immediate payment confirmation online renewal does.

## What if your Louisiana license has already expired?

The board's own licensing FAQ lays out a tiered process based on how long it's been expired:

| Time expired | What you need to do |
|---|---|
| Less than 1 year | Renew online through the standard system; a late fee applies |
| Less than 3 years (36 months) | Restore the license by paying the renewal fee plus applicable late penalties |
| More than 3 years (36 months) | Pass a restoration exam before the board will reinstate the license |

That three-year cutoff is the one to watch. Under three years, it's a matter of paying more money. Past three years, Louisiana treats you close to a new applicant again, requiring a restoration exam rather than just a payment, so a lapsed Louisiana license is far more expensive to fix the longer it sits.

## How does Louisiana's licensing structure connect to renewal?

Louisiana requires different training hours depending on which license you're renewing: 1,500 hours in an approved cosmetology curriculum for a cosmetologist license, 750 hours for an esthetician license, and 500 hours for a manicurist license, all completed before initial licensure and verified by the state board exam. None of that hour requirement repeats at renewal time, since renewal only covers keeping an already-issued license current, not re-proving your original training. It's worth knowing anyway if you're moving between license types or considering adding a second Louisiana license, since each one has its own hour and exam requirement, separate from your existing renewal cycle.

## What should you watch for with an annual cycle?

The main risk with Louisiana's annual renewal isn't the process itself, since it's genuinely one of the simpler state renewal processes we've reviewed. It's remembering to do it every single year instead of every other year. Set a recurring annual reminder tied to your specific license's expiration date, and renew inside that 56-day window rather than waiting until the deadline, since the board's own system won't even accept the payment before that window opens.

## FAQ

**How often do I renew a Louisiana cosmetology license?**
Every year. Louisiana renews cosmetologist, esthetician, manicurist, and instructor licenses annually, unlike the two-year cycle most states use.

**How much does Louisiana cosmetology license renewal cost?**
$35 as of the fee effective August 1, 2025, per a licensing fee tracker's published figure. Confirm the current amount on the board's own renewal form before paying.

**Can I renew my Louisiana license entirely online?**
Yes, through PayStar, a third-party payment processor linked from the Louisiana State Board of Cosmetology's own website. You'll need your license number and a Visa, MasterCard, American Express, or Discover card.

**What happens if my Louisiana license has been expired for years?**
Under one year, you renew online with a late fee. Under three years, you can restore it by paying the renewal fee plus penalties. Past three years, you must pass a restoration exam before the board will reinstate it.

**Does Louisiana require continuing education to renew?**
No. Louisiana's cosmetology renewal requires the fee only, with no continuing education hours attached, unlike states such as Texas and Georgia.
