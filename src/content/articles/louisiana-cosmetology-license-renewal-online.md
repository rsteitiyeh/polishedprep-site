You can renew a Louisiana cosmetology license online through the Louisiana State Board of Cosmetology's payment portal at lsbc.louisiana.gov (the "Pay License Renewal Online" option). Louisiana renews individual licenses annually, requires no continuing education for standard renewal, and the base fee is modest, around $25, though fees increased under a 2024 law, so confirm the current amount on the LSBC site before paying.

Louisiana is one of the simpler states to renew in, and also one of the easier states to get tripped up in, because the board's site splits information across PDFs and the fee amounts changed recently. This walk-through pulls it into one place and tells you exactly where each fact came from.

How to renew online, step by step

1.  Go to lsbc.louisiana.gov and choose the online payment / license renewal option (OnlinePay.aspx).

2.  Enter your license number and identifying details exactly as they appear on your current license. Mismatched names are the most common lookup failure; use your licensed name, not a new married name you haven't filed with the board.

3.  Review the license record the system pulls up. If your address changed, update it; board notices and cards go to the address on file.

4.  Pay the renewal fee by card and keep the confirmation number. If your renewal is past due, expect a delinquent penalty added automatically.

5.  Print or save your receipt. Your updated license follows; the receipt is your proof in the meantime.

The whole thing takes under ten minutes when your record is clean. If the portal can't find your license or shows a status you don't expect, call the board office in Baton Rouge (866-257-7901 toll free, listed on their site) rather than paying against a wrong record.

Special situations

Salon and shop licenses

Salon registrations renew separately from your individual license, with their own fees. If you own your booth or shop, you're tracking two renewals, not one. The board's same online portal handles both, but they are distinct transactions.

Lapsed for more than a renewal cycle

A long lapse can push you from "renew with penalty" into reinstatement territory, and in some cases back toward examination. Troubleshooting the LSBC portal

The board's online payment system is functional but dated, and three failure modes account for nearly every stuck renewal readers report. License lookup fails: you're probably entering a name that doesn't match board records exactly, often after a marriage or a typo in the original filing; call the board rather than guessing repeatedly. Payment goes through but no confirmation arrives: check spam first, then call with your card statement handy, and don't pay twice. Status shows something unexpected, like a hold or an old delinquency: that's a records conversation only board staff can resolve, and the toll-free line (866-257-7901) is genuinely answered during business hours, which is not something every state board can say.

Do your renewal on a computer rather than a phone if you can. The forms predate mobile design, and a mistyped license number on a small screen is the root cause of half these problems.

A note on Louisiana's inspection culture

Louisiana pairs its light renewal requirements with active salon inspections, and inspectors check licenses on the wall along with sanitation practice. A current license physically displayed is the rule, so when you renew online, make sure the board has your correct salon address for delivery of the new credential, and keep your payment receipt until the new license arrives. If an inspector visits during the gap between payment and delivery, the receipt is your friend.

This is also the practical reason not to let renewal slide into the delinquent window: an inspector doesn't care that you intended to renew, and neither does the fine schedule. Annual fee, annual reminder, done.

Put the annual cycle to work for you

One habit turns Louisiana's short cycle from a nuisance into an advantage: pair the renewal with your own annual review. When the calendar reminder fires, renew, then spend thirty minutes checking the three things that quietly drift over a year of salon work: your address and contact details with the board, your displayed license and receipts folder, and your sanitation habits against the current rules. Licensees who bundle these into one yearly session tell us inspections stopped being stressful, because nothing on the inspector's checklist was ever more than twelve months stale.

Renewing multiple credentials

Plenty of Louisiana professionals hold more than one board credential: cosmetologist plus esthetician, an instructor license, or a salon registration on top of the individual license. Each renews separately with its own fee, and the online portal processes them as separate transactions. Instructors should double-check their instructor credential's requirements specifically, since instructor licenses carry different rules in several categories. When in doubt, one call to the board with your license numbers sorts out exactly what's due and when, which beats reverse-engineering it from PDFs.

FAQ

How much does it cost to renew a cosmetology license in Louisiana?

The base individual renewal has historically been about $25 per year, but Louisiana enacted a fee increase in 2024 (announced on lsbc.louisiana.gov), so treat that number as approximate. The online portal shows your exact total, including any delinquent penalty, before you pay.

Does Louisiana require continuing education for cosmetology renewal?

No. Standard individual license renewal in Louisiana has no CE requirement; you renew by paying the annual fee. That's unusual nationally, where 2-year cycles with a few CE hours are the common pattern.

Can I renew my Louisiana cosmetology license if it expired last year?

Usually yes, with a delinquent penalty added to the fee, but a long enough lapse can require reinstatement instead of simple renewal. Check your license status through the LSBC portal or call the board at 866-257-7901 before working, since practicing on an expired license risks fines.

Where do I renew: the state portal or a third-party site?

Only through the board's own site, lsbc.louisiana.gov. Third-party "renewal service" sites charge markups for filling in the same form and sometimes miss deadline nuances. The board's online payment page is the only place your money certainly reaches your license record.

Do I need to display my renewed license in the salon?

Yes. Louisiana requires the current license displayed at your work station, and inspectors check it. After renewing online, keep your payment receipt visible or on hand until the new credential arrives at the address on file, and update that address with the board first if you've moved salons since your last renewal.

Bottom line

Renew at lsbc.louisiana.gov, budget roughly $25 plus any recent fee increase, and put next year's date in your calendar now, because Louisiana's annual cycle is the shortest wait between renewals in the region. Ten minutes online beats a delinquent penalty every time.
