TDLR cosmetology license renewal happens every two years: complete 4 hours of department-approved continuing education, then renew online through the Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation and pay the renewal fee. Licensees who have held a license 15 or more years only need 1 hour of sanitation CE. Details and current fees live at tdlr.texas.gov.

Texas moved cosmetology under TDLR years ago and has been consolidating barbering and cosmetology rules since, so older advice you find in forums is often outdated. Everything below is checked against TDLR's own pages as of July 2026; where a number can change, we say so and point you to the source.

Two of those rows are worth reading twice. The 15-year exemption surprises a lot of veteran stylists who keep buying 4-hour packages they don't need. And the 50-minute hour matters when you're comparing CE providers, because a "4-hour course" is really 200 minutes of instruction.

How to renew your TDLR cosmetology license, step by step

1.  Finish your CE first. TDLR requires 4 hours from a department-approved provider (1 hour sanitation-only if you've been licensed 15+ years). Providers report your completion to TDLR within 7 days, so don't renew the same hour you finish the course.

2.  Confirm your CE posted. Check your license record through TDLR's online licensing portal before paying.

3.  Log in to TDLR's online services at tdlr.texas.gov and select the renewal option for your cosmetology license.

4.  Verify your mailing address and personal details. Wrong addresses are the top reason paper cards and notices go missing.

5.  Pay the renewal fee by card. Save the confirmation page.

6.  Download or print your updated license. Texas lets you verify any license publicly, which is what most salons check anyway.

Choosing a CE provider without overpaying

TDLR publishes its list of approved continuing education providers, and approval is the only credential that matters; a $60 course teaches the same required content as a $18 one. When comparing, check three things: the provider appears on TDLR's approved list, the course covers the current required topics (sanitation content is the core), and the provider reports completions to TDLR electronically. That last one is what makes your renewal smooth, since providers must file within 7 days and the good ones file same-day. Reader-reported prices for the standard 4-hour online package have run $15 to $25 for years, so treat anything above $30 as paying for convenience you can get cheaper.

Veterans with 15+ years licensed: search specifically for 1-hour sanitation courses. They exist, they're cheap, and buying the 4-hour bundle out of habit wastes both money and an evening.

Timing your renewal right

TDLR sends renewal notices ahead of your expiration date, but the notice is a courtesy, not a requirement; the expiration date on your license is your responsibility either way. The sequence that never fails: finish CE at least two weeks before expiration, verify the hours posted to your record through TDLR's online lookup, then renew online the same week. That buffer absorbs the two things that actually go wrong, which are slow provider reporting and finding out your mailing address or name on file is stale. Renewing months early doesn't cost you time, either; your new cycle runs from your expiration date, not your payment date.

Booth renters, one extra note: your individual operator license and any establishment-level credentials are separate renewals with separate dates. Salon owners juggling several licenses should put every expiration in one calendar, because TDLR treats each credential independently and one lapsed license doesn't pause the others.

Working while expired: what's actually at stake

Practicing cosmetology in Texas on an expired license is a violation that can draw administrative penalties, and TDLR does run enforcement, with disciplinary actions published on its site. The fine exposure dwarfs the roughly $50 renewal fee many times over, and a disciplinary record follows your license into every state you might later move to. If you discover you've lapsed, stop taking clients, renew or contact TDLR that day, and document when you stopped working. The gap on your calendar is a far smaller problem than a citation.

FAQ

How many CE hours does TDLR require for cosmetology renewal?

4 hours of department-approved continuing education every two-year renewal cycle. Licensees who have held their license for 15 or more years need only 1 hour of approved sanitation CE. One CE hour equals 50 minutes of instruction. Source: tdlr.texas.gov/barbering-and-cosmetology/individuals/continuing-education.htm.

How much does it cost to renew a cosmetology license in Texas?

Plan on roughly $50 for the state renewal fee (confirm the current amount on TDLR's fee schedule, since fees change by rule) plus $15 to $25 for a typical approved online CE course. On-time renewal all-in usually lands under $80.

Can I renew my TDLR license if my CE isn't done?

No. CE completion is a renewal condition, and providers report your hours to TDLR electronically within 7 days of completion. Finish the course, confirm the hours posted to your record, then renew. Renewing with unposted hours is the most common cause of stuck applications we hear about.

What happens if I let my Texas cosmetology license expire?

You can typically still renew for a period after expiration by paying an increased fee, but you cannot legally work on an expired license. After a longer lapse, TDLR may require a new application. Check your exact status and options through the license search at tdlr.texas.gov before booking clients.

Can I do my Texas CE hours in person instead of online?

Yes. TDLR's approved provider list includes both classroom and online options, and the 4 required hours count the same either way. In-person classes cost more and take a scheduled block of your day, but some licensees prefer them for the hands-on refreshers. Whichever format you choose, confirm the provider's TDLR approval number before paying.

Bottom line

Renewing with TDLR is a two-step errand: 4 CE hours (or 1 sanitation hour for 15-year veterans), then the online renewal and fee at tdlr.texas.gov. Do the CE at least a week before your expiration date so the hours post in time, and the whole thing costs under $80 and less than an hour of your life.
