A TDLR cosmetology license renews every two years for $50 if you file on time, $75 if you're less than 18 months late, and $100 if you're 18 months to three years past expiration. As of September 1, 2025, Texas also requires continuing education hours before you renew, a change from the CE-free renewal Texas cosmetologists had for years. Renewal is filed online through TDLR's licensing system, not by mail.

Texas ties the fee and the paperwork to how long your license has been expired, and the CE rule now applies on top of that. Get either one wrong and TDLR can bounce the renewal back or, past the three-year mark, require you to relicense from scratch: new application, new exam, no shortcuts.

## How much does TDLR cosmetology license renewal cost?

A cosmetology license renewal in Texas costs $50 if it's filed before or on the expiration date. Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation posted this fee schedule effective September 1, 2023, and it remains the current schedule as of this writing ([TDLR fee changes](https://www.tdlr.texas.gov/barbering-and-cosmetology/feechanges.htm)).

| Renewal timing | Fee |
|---|---|
| On or before expiration | $50 |
| Expired less than 18 months | $75 |
| Expired 18 months to 3 years | $100 |
| Expired more than 3 years | Cannot renew; must meet initial licensing requirements again |

The fee is the same across TDLR's cosmetology practitioner categories (Operator, Esthetician, Manicurist) under the current published schedule. TDLR doesn't publish a separate discount for renewing early inside the on-time window, so there's no cost benefit to renewing the day your license expires versus three months out. The only cost benefit to renewing early is avoiding the late tiers entirely.

## Do I need continuing education hours to renew?

Yes, starting September 1, 2025. Before that date, Texas cosmetologists renewed without any CE requirement, unlike many other states. That changed with a new TDLR rule that ties CE hours to how long you've held your license ([TDLR CE requirements](https://www.tdlr.texas.gov/barbering-and-cosmetology/individuals/continuing-education.htm), [TDLR announcement, June 16, 2025](https://www.tdlr.texas.gov/news/2025/06/16/new-continuing-education-requirements-effective-september-1-2025/)).

- **Licensed fewer than 15 years:** 4 CE hours per 2-year renewal cycle, broken down as 1 hour of sanitation, 2 hours of barbering or cosmetology-related content, and 1 hour of human trafficking prevention.
- **Licensed 15 years or more:** 2 CE hours per cycle, 1 hour of sanitation and 1 hour of human trafficking prevention.
- **Dual-licensed (barber and cosmetology):** CE completed under one license satisfies the requirement for both, so you don't have to double up.

This is a newer rule than most cosmetologists who've been renewing on autopilot expect, and it's the single most common reason a renewal gets flagged in 2026: the applicant assumes Texas still has no CE requirement.

## How do I actually renew a TDLR cosmetology license?

Renew through TDLR's online licensing system, not by phone or mail. Go to TDLR's [renewal page](https://www.tdlr.texas.gov/barbering-and-cosmetology/individuals/renew/), answer the short questionnaire about your license type and current status (active, expired under 90 days, expired under 180 days, or expired up to 3 years), and it routes you into the online application at tdlr.texas.gov/App_Online/. TDLR doesn't operate a distinct mail-in renewal channel on its official pages; the agency's guidance pushes applicants to the online system. If you have questions about a specific renewal, TDLR's licensing line is (800) 803-9202 or (512) 463-6599.

Have these ready before you start:

1. Your current or expired TDLR license number
2. A valid payment method for the renewal fee
3. Proof of completed CE hours if you're within the new requirement window
4. Updated contact information (TDLR renewal notices go out by mail and email, so an old address is a common reason renewals get missed)

## What happens if my license has been expired for years?

Once a Texas cosmetology license has been expired for more than three years, TDLR will not renew it. At that point the agency treats you as a new applicant: you file the initial cosmetology license application, meet the current training-hour requirement, and sit for both the written and practical exams again through TDLR's exam vendor, PSI. There's no partial credit for the years you were previously licensed. This is the costliest mistake a lapsed licensee can make, and it's the reason TDLR's own renewal questionnaire asks how long your license has been expired before it routes you anywhere.

## Bottom line

Renew before the expiration date if you can. It's $50, it avoids the CE-hour math getting more urgent, and it keeps you off the clock that eventually forces a full relicense. If you've already let it lapse, check exactly how many months it's been before you start the online application, since the fee and the paperwork both change at the 18-month and 3-year marks.

## FAQ

**Is TDLR cosmetology CE actually required now, or is that only for barbers?**
It applies to both. Effective September 1, 2025, TDLR requires CE hours for cosmetology license renewal, not just barbering. The hour count depends on how long you've held the license: 4 hours if under 15 years, 2 hours if 15 years or more.

**Can I renew my TDLR cosmetology license by mail?**
TDLR's official renewal process routes applicants through its online licensing system. The agency's published renewal guidance does not list a mail-in option, so confirm directly with TDLR at (800) 803-9202 if you have a reason you can't renew online.

**What happens if I renew late?**
The fee increases in tiers: $75 if you're under 18 months expired, $100 if you're 18 months to 3 years expired. Past 3 years, TDLR requires a full new application, training-hour proof, and both exams again.

**Does CE from my barber license count toward my cosmetology renewal?**
Yes, if you hold both licenses. TDLR allows CE completed under one license to satisfy the requirement for the other, so dual-licensed practitioners don't need separate CE hours for each.

**How often does a TDLR cosmetology license need to be renewed?**
Every two years. TDLR mails and emails renewal notices ahead of the expiration date, which is why keeping your contact information current with the agency matters.
