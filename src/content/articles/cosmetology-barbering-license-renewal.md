Cosmetology and barbering license renewal is handled entirely by your state board, not a national body, so the cycle length, fee, and continuing education rules depend on where you're licensed. Most states run a two year renewal cycle, some require continuing education hours and some don't, and a handful, like Louisiana, renew annually instead. The board that licensed you always sets the deadline, the fee, and the reinstatement penalty if you miss it.

That single fact, that renewal is a state function and not a national one, explains almost every question people have about it. Below, we break down the general pattern, three states as worked examples, what happens if you let a license lapse, and where to check your own state's specific deadline.

## How often do you renew a cosmetology or barbering license?

Most states put licensees on a two year renewal cycle, though a few states renew annually and a few use other intervals tied to your birth month or original license date. Texas, for example, issues barbering and cosmetology licenses for two years at a time, and the Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation sends a renewal notice about 60 days before the license expires, though the department is explicit that renewing on time is the licensee's responsibility regardless of whether that notice arrives.

Louisiana is the clear outlier among states we checked: the Louisiana State Board of Cosmetology renews cosmetologist, esthetician, manicurist, and instructor licenses every year, not every two years, which means Louisiana licensees pay a smaller fee more often rather than a larger fee every other year.

## Do you need continuing education to renew?

It depends entirely on your state, and the split is close to even. Texas requires 4 hours of department approved continuing education every two years to renew a cosmetology or barber license, dropping to 2 hours if you've held the license at least 15 years; barber licensees specifically became subject to this requirement as of September 1, 2025, after a prior exemption. Georgia's cosmetology and barber board waives continuing education on your very first renewal after licensure, then requires 5 hours every two year cycle after that, split between a required health and safety course and board approved electives. Louisiana, by contrast, requires no continuing education at all to renew, just the fee.

## How do the numbers actually compare across states?

Here's what we found checking three state boards directly:

| State | Renewal cycle | Renewal fee (per license type) | Continuing education | Online portal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Texas | 2 years | $50 on time | 4 hrs/2 yrs (2 hrs if 15+ yrs licensed) | TDLR online renewal |
| Georgia | 2 years | ~$45 for nail tech; varies by license type | 5 hrs/2 yrs, waived first renewal | GOALS portal |

Fees above are per license type and change periodically, so treat the table as a pattern, not a quote, and confirm your exact fee on your board's current fee schedule before you pay.

## What happens if your license expires?

Nearly every state gives you a grace period with a late fee before the license lapses entirely, and a longer, harder deadline after which you need more than a late payment to get back in good standing. Louisiana's board will let you renew online with a late fee if your license has been expired less than a year, restore it with the fee plus penalties if it's been expired less than three years, and requires you to pass a restoration exam if it's been expired more than three years, essentially treating you like a new applicant again. Georgia's nail technician license moves into a lapsed status just 30 days after the renewal deadline, after which you need a full reinstatement application rather than a simple late renewal.

The pattern holds across most states we've checked: short grace period with a fee, a longer window where restoration is still possible but costs more, and a hard cutoff where the state effectively makes you retest. Don't let a license drift past that middle window if you can help it. The retest requirement is the expensive outcome, not the late fee.

## What does the renewal process actually look like?

Almost every state now runs renewal through an online licensing portal tied to your original application, where you confirm your contact information, attest to any continuing education completed, disclose any new convictions or disciplinary actions, and pay by card. Texas runs this through its own TDLR online system, Georgia through the Secretary of State's GOALS portal, and Louisiana through a third party payment processor called PayStar linked from the board's own site. Paper renewal by mail is usually still an option everywhere, just slower, typically requiring a check or money order and adding a mailing processing fee on top of the base renewal fee.

Set a calendar reminder for 60 to 90 days before your specific expiration date, since that's when most boards open the renewal window and when notices, if you get one, typically go out. Waiting for the notice email is a bad plan. Boards themselves say the renewal deadline is on the licensee, not on whether a reminder arrives in your inbox.

## FAQ

**How often do I have to renew a cosmetology or barbering license?**
Most states use a two year cycle, but check your specific board. Louisiana renews annually, which is the main exception we found among states with clear published cycles.

**Do all states require continuing education to renew?**
No. Texas and Georgia both require continuing education hours on a recurring basis (Georgia waives it on your first renewal), while Louisiana requires none at all, just the renewal fee.

**What happens if I miss my renewal deadline?**
Most states offer a short grace period with a late fee, followed by a longer window where you can still restore the license for a bigger penalty, and eventually a hard cutoff where you have to retest. Louisiana's cutoff is 3 years; Georgia's nail technician license lapses after just 30 days late.

**Can I renew my license online?**
In every state we checked, yes. Texas and Georgia use their own state licensing portals; Louisiana routes online payment through a third party processor called PayStar linked from the board's site. Mail-in renewal is usually still available as a slower alternative.

**Does the renewal fee cover both a cosmetology and a barbering license if I hold both?**
No. Renewal fees are per license type, so a cosmetologist, barber, esthetician, manicurist, and instructor license each renew and get billed separately, even if the same person holds more than one.
