Georgia nail license renewal runs through the GOALS online portal (goals.sos.ga.gov) under the State Board of Cosmetology and Barbers. For the current cycle, renewals opened July 1, 2026, licenses expire September 30, 2026, and the late renewal window runs October 1 through November 15, 2026. Continuing education completion is tracked through CE Broker. Source: sos.ga.gov.

Georgia is unusual in expiring licenses statewide on the same date rather than by birthday or issue date, which makes the deadline easy to remember and easy to miss all at once: every nail tech in the state is renewing in the same three-month window. Here's the whole process, verified against the Secretary of State's cosmetology board pages.

How to renew in GOALS, step by step

1.  Complete your continuing education first and confirm it's recorded in CE Broker, the tracking system the board uses. Renewals check against CE Broker records, so unreported hours stall everything.

2.  Log in at goals.sos.ga.gov. First-time GOALS users need to claim their license record; have your license number and the email the board has on file.

3.  Select your nail technician license and choose renew. Verify your name, address, and employment details while you're in there.

4.  Attest to the renewal questions honestly. Discipline and criminal-history questions route some applications to manual review, which is slower but not fatal; lying is.

5.  Pay the renewal fee by card (on the order of $45; confirm the exact current amount in GOALS, since the checkout screen is authoritative). Late renewals show the penalty in the total automatically.

6.  Save your confirmation and check your license status a few days later in the public lookup.

CE: the step people actually miss

Georgia tracks CE through CE Broker, and the practical consequence is that finishing a course isn't enough; the hours must appear in CE Broker before your renewal processes cleanly. Approved providers usually report for you within days, but verify your CE Broker account shows the hours before you sit down to renew. The board's site links the current hour requirements for each license type at sos.ga.gov; check yours there rather than trusting a course vendor's marketing page, because vendors have every incentive to sell you more hours than the board requires.

Setting up CE Broker without the confusion

CE Broker offers a free basic account, and that's all a Georgia nail tech needs; the paid tiers add convenience features, not compliance. Create the free account, link it to your license number, and check it after every course you complete. Approved providers report electronically, usually within a few days, but the licensee carries the responsibility either way, so keep your course completion certificates until the hours show up. If a provider hasn't reported after a week, contact them with your certificate in hand; that nudge resolves nearly every case. What you're avoiding is the worst-case timing: discovering unreported hours on September 28 with the statewide deadline two days out and the provider's support queue full of everyone else in the same spot.

First time in GOALS: claiming your record

Georgia migrated licensing to the GOALS system, and licensees who haven't logged in since the migration need to claim their existing license before they can renew. The claim process matches you to your record using your license number and personal details, then ties the record to your email login. Do this in July, not September, for one simple reason: if the matching fails (name changes and old email addresses are the usual culprits), the fix runs through board staff, and board response times stretch precisely when everyone in the state is renewing at once. A five-minute July login surfaces any records problem while there's still ample time to fix it.

While you're in there, update your mailing address and employment information. Georgia's board communicates through the contact details on file, and inspectors cross-check salon rosters against license records.

New to Georgia from another state? Note that your old state's cycle habits don't transfer. If you came from a birthday-cycle state, the statewide September 30 date will feel arbitrary, and it catches transplants disproportionately. Your GOALS record shows your true expiration; trust it over instinct.

Salon owners: the September stack-up

Because Georgia expires everything on the same statewide date, a salon owner's September involves every tech's individual license plus the shop's own registration renewing simultaneously. The owners who handle it well run it like inventory: one spreadsheet, every license number, CE status checked in August, renewals done the first week the window opens. The ones who don't discover in October that a booth renter lapsed, which becomes the salon's inspection problem too. If that's you, send this page to your techs in June; the statewide date means one reminder covers everybody, which is the single upside of Georgia's system.

FAQ

When does my Georgia nail license expire?

For the current cycle, September 30, 2026, statewide, with renewals open since July 1, 2026 and a late window through November 15, 2026, per sos.ga.gov. Georgia expires cosmetology-board licenses on a common date rather than by birthday, so every Georgia nail tech shares your deadline.

How much does Georgia nail license renewal cost?

On-time renewal runs on the order of $45, roughly doubling with the late penalty, though the GOALS checkout screen shows the exact current amount and is the figure that counts. Reinstatement after the late window costs more still and adds paperwork.

Does Georgia require continuing education for nail techs?

Yes, and completion is tracked through CE Broker, which the board checks at renewal. The current required hours for each license class are posted on the board's pages at sos.ga.gov. Use an approved provider and confirm the hours appear in your CE Broker account before renewing.

What if I miss the November 15 late deadline?

Simple renewal closes and you're into reinstatement with the board, which means more fees, more forms, and possible additional requirements depending on how long you've lapsed. You also can't legally work in the meantime. If you're already past the line, contact the board through sos.ga.gov promptly; the problem only compounds.

Can I renew by mail instead of using GOALS?

Georgia has moved licensing functions into the GOALS portal, and online renewal is the standard path the board directs licensees to at sos.ga.gov. If you genuinely can't use the portal, contact the board through the site's contact options well before the deadline; waiting until late September to raise a technical problem leaves no slack for a fix.

Bottom line

Renew in GOALS the week the window opens: CE confirmed in CE Broker, ten minutes at goals.sos.ga.gov, roughly $45, done until 2028. The September 30 expiration and November 15 late cutoff are hard dates from sos.ga.gov, so put both in your phone now and beat the statewide rush.
