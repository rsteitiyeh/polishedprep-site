Georgia nail technician license renewal is due by August 31 of odd-numbered years, handled entirely online through the state's GOALS licensing portal, and requires a renewal fee plus, after your first renewal cycle, five hours of continuing education. Miss the deadline and you get a 30-day late window before the license lapses into a status that requires full reinstatement instead of a simple renewal.

The Georgia State Board of Cosmetology and Barbers, under the Georgia Secretary of State's licensing division, oversees nail technician licensure alongside cosmetologist, esthetician, hair designer, and barber licenses, and each license type renews on its own schedule and fee, even for someone who holds more than one.

## When does a Georgia nail technician license expire?

August 31 of odd-numbered years, so 2027, 2029, and so on. The Georgia Secretary of State's own licensing guide is explicit that you should apply for renewal before that date to avoid late fees, and that instructors, a related but separate license type, renew on a different date, June 30 instead of August 31.

## How much does Georgia nail license renewal cost?

Georgia's renewal fee schedule is published live on the GOALS portal itself and changes periodically, so confirm the current figure there before you pay. As of the most recent published cycle, a Georgia Nail Technician renewal runs $45, plus a processing fee of $5 for online applications or $10 for mailed paper applications, according to a Georgia-focused continuing education provider that tracks the board's fee schedule cycle to cycle. Late renewal, filed within the 30-day grace window after the deadline, adds a $90 late fee on top of the base renewal fee.

| Renewal scenario | Fee |
|---|---|
| On-time renewal | ~$45 renewal fee + $5 (online) / $10 (mail) processing |
| Late renewal (within 30 days) | ~$45 renewal fee + $90 late fee + processing fee |
| Reinstatement (lapsed beyond 30 days) | $200 reinstatement application fee + processing fee |

## Do you need continuing education to renew?

Not on your very first renewal after getting licensed. The board waives continuing education for a first-time licensee's initial renewal cycle. Every renewal after that requires five hours of board-approved continuing education: three hours specifically in a health and safety course, and two hours in any other course approved by the Georgia board, which can include nail-technique specific content. You're generally not required to submit proof of completion unless the board selects you for an audit, at which point you'll need your completion certificate on hand.

## How do you actually renew through the GOALS portal?

1. Log into the GOALS portal at goals.sos.ga.gov using the account tied to your original license application.
2. Confirm your contact information is current, since the board communicates renewal reminders and any deficiency notices by email.
3. Answer the renewal screening questions: whether you've had a conviction or board sanction since your last renewal, whether you've completed your required continuing education, and whether you're a U.S. citizen.
4. Pay the renewal fee plus the processing fee by card.
5. Allow roughly 15 business days for the board to process your renewal application.
6. Once processed, log back into GOALS to print your updated license.

The renewal window typically opens around three months before your expiration date, so for the August 31 deadline that's roughly June 1. Opening the window early doesn't move your expiration date up, it just gives you more time to handle the process before the deadline pressure sets in.

## What happens if you miss the deadline?

You have a 30-day late window after August 31 where you can still renew through the online portal or a paper application, but you can't legally practice until the renewal is actually processed. Miss that 30-day window entirely and the license moves into a lapsed status, which requires a full reinstatement application rather than a late renewal, plus documentation of continuing education for every renewal cycle you missed and a $200 reinstatement fee.

Don't let a Georgia nail license drift past that 30-day mark if you're actively working. The gap between "pay a late fee" and "file a reinstatement application with a $200 fee and back-dated CE proof" is the single most expensive mistake in this entire process, and it's entirely avoidable with a calendar reminder.

## FAQ

**When is Georgia nail technician license renewal due?**
August 31 of odd-numbered years (2025, 2027, 2029, and so on). Nail technician instructors renew on a different date, June 30.

**Do I need continuing education to renew my Georgia nail license?**
Not on your first renewal after initial licensure, which is waived. Every renewal cycle after that requires five hours, including a required three-hour health and safety course.

**What happens if I renew late in Georgia?**
You have a 30-day grace period after the deadline where you can still renew online or by mail with a late fee, but you can't practice until it's processed. After 30 days, the license lapses and requires a full reinstatement application instead.

**How do I renew a Georgia nail technician license online?**
Through the GOALS portal at goals.sos.ga.gov, using the account tied to your original license. You'll confirm your contact info, answer a few screening questions, and pay by card.

**Is the renewal fee the same for every Georgia cosmetology license type?**
No. Nail technician, cosmetologist, esthetician, hair designer, and barber licenses each carry their own fee and renewal deadline, even when the same person holds more than one license type.
