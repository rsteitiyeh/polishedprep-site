A cosmetology state board kit is the collection of implements, supplies, and disinfection materials you bring to a practical licensing exam so you can perform the services examiners will grade: things like a mannequin head and stand, cutting and styling tools, and infection control supplies. The exact required list is state and testing vendor specific and lives in your candidate information bulletin, not in a universal packing list, so what's below is the pattern every state's kit follows, not a substitute for your own bulletin.

Written exams don't require a kit at all, since they're multiple choice on a computer. It's the hands on practical exam, where you're graded on live technique, that requires you to show up with your own tools.

## Why does the required kit vary by state?

Because each state administers its practical exam a little differently, even when the theory exam behind it is the same NIC national test used in most states. The National-Interstate Council of State Boards of Cosmetology develops the content outline and sample questions in its Candidate Information Bulletins, but each state or its testing vendor, commonly PSI or Prov, publishes its own specific list of allowed and required kit items, labeling rules, and disqualifying substitutions for the practical portion. That's why a kit built for Texas's PSI administered practical won't necessarily match one built for a state using a different vendor.

Always pull your own state's current candidate information bulletin before you buy or pack anything. Kit requirements get updated, and testing centers turn candidates away over missing or non-compliant items more often than almost anything else on exam day.

## What's typically in a cosmetology state board kit?

Across the states and vendors we checked, practical exam kits cluster around the same core categories, matching the tool categories NIC's own theory exam content outline tests candidates on for hair, skin, and nail services:

| Category | Typical items |
|---|---|
| Mannequin & model support | Mannequin head, mannequin stand, clamp or table mount |
| Cutting & sectioning | Shears, texturizing shears, straight razor, clips, sectioning combs |
| Styling | Blow dryer, curling and flat irons, rollers, wet-set tools |
| Chemical service tools | Tint brushes, mixing bowls, timer, gloves, cape |
| Nail service tools | Nippers, files, buffers, cuticle pusher, polish |
| Skin service tools (esthetics-adjacent) | Tweezers, spatulas, extractors |
| Infection control | EPA-registered disinfectant, disinfection container, sanitizing spray, disposable towels |
| Draping | Client cape, neck strips, shampoo cape |

A well built kit spans every service category the practical actually tests, not just the ones a candidate personally prefers. Examiners grade against a checklist for each station, and a missing implement at one station can cost you the whole section regardless of how well you performed everything else.

## What's usually not included and needs separate sourcing?

Mannequin heads, mannequin hands, and their stands are commonly sold separately from the rest of a practical exam kit, since candidates often already own one from school or want a specific brand and hair type. Some states also require a live model for certain sections rather than a mannequin, which is a scheduling and consent issue, not a supply one, and shows up in the candidate bulletin under model requirements rather than the kit list.

## What should you double check before exam day?

Confirm three things against your specific state's current bulletin: which items are required versus optional, whether any items need to be a specific brand-neutral type (some states disqualify branded or labeled packaging on exam day), and whether your disinfectant is EPA registered and within the contact time your state's infection control section tests on. A kit that's technically complete but uses an expired or mislabeled disinfectant container is a common, entirely avoidable way to lose points on the safety portion of a practical.

Pack your kit a few days ahead, not the morning of, so you have time to replace anything missing. Test centers generally don't sell backup supplies, and arriving without a required item usually means you can't complete that section of the exam at all.

## FAQ

**Do I need a kit for the written cosmetology exam?**
No. The written theory exam is multiple choice, usually computer based, and requires no implements at all. Kits are only for the hands on practical exam.

**Is the kit list the same in every state?**
No. The National-Interstate Council of State Boards of Cosmetology sets the content outline for the theory exam used in most states, but each state or its testing vendor publishes its own specific practical exam kit and labeling requirements in its candidate information bulletin.

**Do I need to buy a mannequin head separately?**
Often, yes. Mannequin heads, hands, and stands are commonly excluded from prepackaged practical exam kits and sold on their own, partly because many candidates already have one from cosmetology school.

**What's the most common reason a candidate loses points related to their kit?**
Missing or improperly maintained infection control supplies, especially an EPA registered disinfectant that isn't used for its full listed contact time, since nearly every state grades safety and sanitation as a separate, heavily weighted category.

**Where do I find my state's exact required kit list?**
In your state's current candidate information bulletin, published by your state board or its testing vendor. The National-Interstate Council of State Boards of Cosmetology maintains a repository of current bulletins by state.
